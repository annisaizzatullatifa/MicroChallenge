our story

Bali, also known as the Island of the Gods or the Lesser Sunda Islands, is a province located in Indonesia. The capital city is Denpasar. Bali is situated in the western part of the Nusa Tenggara Archipelago. At the beginning of Indonesia's independence, the island was part of the Sunda Kecil Province with its capital in Singaraja. Today, the archipelago is divided into three provinces: Bali, Nusa Tenggara Barat (West Nusa Tenggara), and Nusa Tenggara Timur (East Nusa Tenggara).
